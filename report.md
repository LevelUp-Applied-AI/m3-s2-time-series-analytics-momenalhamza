# Executive Report — Time-Series Analytics

## Revenue Trends

Over the 12-month period, revenue demonstrated strong overall growth with noticeable acceleration toward the end of 2025. Monthly revenue increased consistently from April through December, with December representing the peak performance period.

This growth was driven by both:

* An increase in order volume (from ~763 to over 5400 orders)
* A rise in total revenue (reaching ~1.87M in December)

A significant drop occurred in January 2026 (~-58% revenue decline), followed by a recovery in February and a strong rebound in March (+61%). This suggests seasonality or post-holiday slowdown followed by renewed demand.

Key insight:
Growth is primarily volume-driven, not just price-driven, as both orders and revenue moved together.

---

## Customer Retention

Customer retention varies significantly across cohorts:

* Strongest cohorts: November and December 2025

  * ~66% retention within 30 days
  * ~93–98% within 90 days
* Weakest cohorts: June–August 2025

  * ~38–43% within 30 days

This indicates that customers acquired late in the year are of higher quality or had a better initial experience.

Important note:
Cohorts from 2026 (especially February and March) show artificially high retention (~99%), which is misleading. These cohorts have not had enough time to fully observe 60–90 day behavior, so they should not be used for comparison.

## Category / Segment Performance

Revenue by customer segment shows clear differences:

### Consumer Segment

* Largest contributor to total revenue
* Strong growth in Q4 2025
* Significant drop in January, followed by recovery
* Consistent upward trend overall

### Business Segment

* Stable and steadily growing
* Strong performance in November–December
* Slight dip in January, but recovered quickly

### Enterprise Segment

* More volatile compared to others
* High spikes (e.g., October growth >100%)
* Larger fluctuations month-to-month

Key insight:

* Consumer drives scale
* Business provides stability
* Enterprise introduces volatility but potential high upside

---

## Recommendations

1. Invest in Q4 Acquisition Channels
   Cohorts from November and December show the highest retention. Marketing strategies used during this period should be analyzed and scaled.

2. Improve Mid-Year Customer Experience
   Lower retention in June–August suggests weaker onboarding or customer experience. Focus on improving first-purchase experience during these months.

3. Focus on Repeat Purchase Strategies
   Since revenue growth is strongly tied to repeat orders, implement:

   * Email remarketing
   * Loyalty programs
   * Post-purchase engagement

4. Treat Recent Cohorts Carefully
   Avoid making strategic decisions based on early 2026 cohorts, as retention metrics are not fully matured.

5. Segment-Based Strategy

   * Grow Consumer segment for scale
   * Maintain Business segment for consistency
   * Optimize Enterprise targeting to reduce volatility

---

## Final Insight

The business is growing strongly, especially driven by late-year performance and repeat customer behavior. The biggest opportunity lies in improving retention for mid-year cohorts and doubling down on high-performing acquisition periods.
